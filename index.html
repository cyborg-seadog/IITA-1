PROJECT IDEA

Overview

Application that lets you view what nationalities visit what places abroad. This app will allow users to see hotspots of their chosen nationality filter. You will bring up the app, choose a location from the filters and hit ok, bringing up locations around the world in which people from specific nations have logged in at. The hotspots will be colour coded for ease of use when filtering for different countries.

Motivation

If you are a solo traveller or with some friends going to a new country, it may be a bit overwhelming or confusing exploring a new place. You may be in a new country where English is not the first language and are struggling to get around. This app will help people meet others from their own country or speak the same language, allowing for users to make new connections and feel more comfortable travelling around the foreign land. This can be useful for tourists to visit attractions or vice versa, avoid areas with high volumes of traffic. This can also be a useful tool for refugees who flee from their country with no support, to find others from their native country.

Description

The application will be free to download, however will require that you make an account. The main screen will be a map of the world that you can interact with. At the top left will be a drop-down menu that will display profile, filters for countries, settings and help. The profile link will take you to a sub-menu which will display options for your profile such as changing your profile picture, selecting your home country or country of your choosing, username and a short bio about yourself and an anonymous tab, the anonymous tab will be explained later.

Within the filters tab, you will be able to choose which countries to display on the map in a type of heat signature configuration and choose the map style i.e. topographic, satellite. The heat signatures will be constantly updated by other users of this service and hotspots can change with time for each country. You will be able to choose multiple countries in the filters, so for example, you’re an Australian in a non-English speaking Country and want to find people that speak English or are from Australia, you could set the filter to only display countries that primarily speak English i.e. America, Canada, Britain, Australia and the map will display hotspots where people from those countries have pinged in at.

Users can have the option to anonymously ping to produce a heat signature or ping with their profile and leave a little blurb about the location. Anonymous pinging can be turned on in the profile menu using a slide tab. With the latter option, users can view the profile that has left a message and speak with them directly, however if the person who left the ping does not wish to be contacted, they can turn off receiving messages from other users in the settings menu.

You will be able to download the most recent snapshot of the maps heat signatures beforehand. You can choose specific countries or download them all, from in the past to present. This will be very useful for say a refugee who intends on coming to a different country but has no means of getting internet or mobile service, they could view where the most recent congregation of their home country is and travel to that spot. The settings tab will have options such as origin Country, dark/light theme, info about the app, password change, notification settings and language settings. The help tab will display FAQ and advice on how to use the app.

Tools and tech

I will utilize the Mapbox mobile Software Development Kits (SDK) as well as their geocoding API. The mobile SDK will allow me to customise the maps to be usable on a mobile device, such as a tablet or phone, designate certain touch features to correspond with actions on the application and allow for use on either Android or IOS. Mapboxes geocoding API will allow users to upload their data which will then be transferred onto the map for other to see.

Using the Datasets API specifically is how I intend on creating the software that will allow users to upload their data i.e., country and location as well as a little blurb if they wish. As for the map design itself I will use Mapboxes vector, raster tiles and style API which will aide me in the design, layout and images required. I will be able to use both standard topographic maps and satellite imagery.

As I intend to have this app run on both IOS and Android, I will utilise Transpiler, a tool that will translate one programming language to another. I will also use a cloud service such as Azure or AWS to store data gathered from our users.
